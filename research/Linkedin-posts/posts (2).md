### Post 1 — Zero-Click Content Framework
**Date:** March 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~780 | **Comments:** ~95

**Summary:** Defines zero-click content as content that delivers its full value within the platform — no clicks required. Breaks it into 3 types: Educational (teach something), Entertaining (make them feel something), Provocative (challenge an assumption). Argues this is more strategic than link-based content because it works with the algorithm, builds native reach, and creates platform trust.

**Key insight:** The goal of a LinkedIn post is NOT to drive traffic. It is to make someone think "this person knows their stuff." The click or inquiry comes later, as a result of trust built over time.

### Post 2 — Audience Research Before Content
**Date:** February 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~640 | **Comments:** ~78

**Summary:** Most content marketers write about what they find interesting. Amanda argues you should write about what your audience is actively searching for, talking about, and struggling with. **Date:** February 2026 | **Format:** Carousel (7 slides) | **Likes:** ~920 | **Comments:** ~110

**Summary:** Most marketers create content but don't distribute it. Distribution plays: LinkedIn native post, newsletter embed, Slack community share, comment on a related post, repurpose for other platforms. Final slide: "Creating is 20% of the work. Distribution is 80%."

### Post 4 — The Engagement Asset Strategy
**Date:** January 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~590 | **Comments:** ~68

**Summary:** Build "engagement assets" — a specific PDF, dataset, or framework mentioned in comments before a DM conversation starts. Instead of cold-pitching after someone likes your post, you create a natural conversation bridge: "I mentioned this framework — want me to send you the full version?" Creates warm, permission-based outreach from LinkedIn content.

**Why it matters for B2B SaaS:** Directly connects LinkedIn content to pipeline generation without any pushy sales mechanic.

### Post 5 — The 40% Open Rate Newsletter Strategy
**Date:** December 2025 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~710 | **Comments:** ~85

**Summary:** Why her newsletter gets 40% open rates: 1) Written for one specific person (a CMO at a mid-size B2B company), 2) Never sent without a clear promise in the subject line, 3) Always includes one immediately actionable idea. Most newsletters fail because they are written for the writer, not the reader.

### Post 6 — Content vs. Ads for B2B
**Date:** November 2025 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~830 | **Comments:** ~100

**Summary:** SparkToro research shows B2B buyers increasingly distrust ads and trust creator content and peer recommendations. Organic LinkedIn content functions as a trust infrastructure that makes paid ads more efficient when you do run them. Not either/or — content first, paid amplifies.
