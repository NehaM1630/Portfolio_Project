### Post 1 — The Pathological Empathy Framework
**Date:** March 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~740 | **Comments:** ~88

**Summary:** Great content requires "pathological empathy" — not just knowing your audience's job title, but genuinely understanding their daily frustrations, ambitions, and anxieties. Most B2B content fails because it is written from the company's perspective, not the reader's. The test: read your last post and ask — would the person you're writing for feel like you understand them? Or would they feel sold to?

**Why it matters:** The foundational mindset behind all high-performing content. Before any tactic or framework, this is the starting point.

### Post 2 — Writing Quality in the Age of AI
**Date:** February 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~910 | **Comments:** ~108

**Summary:** As AI-generated content floods every channel, the differentiator is now genuine human voice and original perspective. AI can produce competent sentences — it cannot produce original ideas or authentic experience. The writers and brands that invest in developing a distinct voice and real perspective will stand out clearly in 2026.

**Why it matters:** Directly relevant to B2B SaaS LinkedIn strategy. Generic AI-written posts are increasingly easy to identify and ignore. Human voice is now a competitive advantage.

### Post 3 — The One Sentence Test
**Date:** January 2026 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~680 | **Comments:** ~80

**Summary:** Every piece of content should be able to complete this sentence: "This content will help [specific person] do [specific thing] so they can [specific outcome]." If you cannot fill in all three blanks, you don't have a content brief — you have a topic. Topics don't convert. Briefs do.

### Post 4 — Why Newsletters Outlast Social Media
**Date:** December 2025 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~820 | **Comments:** ~96

**Summary:** LinkedIn algorithms change constantly. Email newsletters don't. Build a newsletter audience as your primary content asset — LinkedIn is a distribution channel, not a destination. The goal is always to move people from "LinkedIn follower" to "email subscriber." LinkedIn is rented land. Email is owned land.

**Key strategic point:** This is why many of the experts in this research collection all run newsletters. LinkedIn builds the audience; email owns the relationship.

### Post 5 — The Writing Habit
**Date:** November 2025 | **Format:** Text post | **Likes:** ~760 | **Comments:** ~90

**Summary:** Writes 500 words every morning before checking email. The habit, not the talent, is what creates consistent content output. "Most content problems are actually consistency problems dressed up as quality problems." If you're not writing every day, you can't improve — and you can't produce enough volume to find what resonates.
