## What the Algorithm Rewards

### 1. Dwell Time Over Speed
How long someone spends reading your post matters more than how fast they like it. A post that keeps 1,000 people reading for 4 minutes will outperform a post that gets 100 quick likes.
**Implication:** Long-form text posts and multi-slide carousels both create dwell time and get rewarded.

### 2. Comments Over Likes
The algorithm weights comments significantly more than likes. A post with 30 thoughtful comments will outperform a post with 300 likes.
**Implication:** Write posts that give people a reason to respond — questions, contrarian takes, relatable scenarios.

### 3. Content Saves
Post saves are the strongest signal that content is genuinely valuable. This metric is now visible in LinkedIn analytics.
**Implication:** Frameworks, checklists, and reference content get saved more than opinion posts.

### 4. ICP Engagement Quality
LinkedIn is now better at identifying whether engagement is coming from relevant professionals or from unrelated accounts. Engagement from your ICP weighs significantly more.
**Implication:** Do not use engagement pods with irrelevant audiences. It signals inauthenticity and actively hurts distribution.

### 5. Deep Expertise Signals
The algorithm increasingly identifies genuine subject matter experts versus people who post about everything. Focused, deep content on a specific topic is rewarded over broad generalist posting.
**Implication:** Own a niche topic and go deep. Posting about "marketing" in general performs worse than posting specifically about "B2B SaaS demand generation."

### 6. Consistency
Regular posting signals reliability to the algorithm. Companies and individuals that post 3–5x per week consistently see 2x more engagement than those who post in bursts.

## Timing and Cadence

- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times:** 7–9am local, 12–1pm local (commute and lunch)
- **Optimal frequency:** 3–5x/week for personal profiles, 2–3x/week for company pages
- **Post lifespan:** LinkedIn now surfaces posts 2–3 weeks old if relevant — evergreen content compounds
- **Reactivation trick:** Comment on your own post 8–24 hours after publishing to re-trigger distribution

## Pre-Publishing Checklist

Before posting anything, check each box:

- [ ] Does line 1 create a reason to keep reading? (Hook test)
- [ ] Is all the value delivered inside the post? (Zero-click test)
- [ ] Is there a natural reason for a relevant person to comment? (Engagement test)
- [ ] Have I removed all external links from the post body? (Algorithm test)
- [ ] Does this reflect genuine expertise or real experience? (Authenticity test)
- [ ] Would I save this post if I saw it from someone else? (Save test)
- [ ] Is this specifically useful for my ICP — or for everyone? (ICP test)

## Forward-Looking: What Changes in 2026

Based on Devin Reed's predictions and LinkedIn data trends:

1. **AI content detection improves** — LinkedIn will increasingly flag AI-generated posts for reduced distribution
2. **Short-form video grows** — especially clips from podcasts, webinars, and conference talks
3. **Employee advocacy > company pages** — personal profile reach will continue to dominate brand page reach
4. **Niche depth rewarded** — algorithm will favor people who consistently post on one focused topic
5. **Comments as content** — well-crafted comments on other people's posts are an increasingly valid reach strategy on their own
