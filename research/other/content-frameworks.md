## Framework 1: The Content Pyramid for B2B SaaS
*Source: Emily Kramer + Devin Reed*

```
        [Tier 1] — Quarterly
         Case Studies
      High effort, high impact

        [Tier 2] — Monthly
      Frameworks & Playbooks
    Medium effort, high impact

       [Tier 3] — 3–5x/week
    LinkedIn Posts & Carousels
     Low effort, variable impact

        [Tier 4] — Daily
      Comments & Engagement
    Very low effort, high leverage
```

**Key insight:** Most SaaS companies start at Tier 3 without ever building Tier 1 or Tier 2. Without the depth underneath, LinkedIn posts have nothing substantial to draw from. The pyramid must be built from the bottom up.

## Framework 2: Zero-Click Content Formula
*Source: Amanda Natividad + Justin Welsh*

Every LinkedIn post should be **self-contained** — delivers 100% of its value without requiring a click to an external link.

Three valid types:
- **Educational** — teaches a specific skill or concept
- **Entertaining** — makes the reader feel something (surprise, recognition, amusement)
- **Provocative** — challenges a widely-held assumption

**The test:** Can someone read your post and immediately apply or share something? If they need to click a link first, you have not delivered the value yet.

**Algorithm note:** External links reduce LinkedIn reach by 20–35%.

## Framework 3: Hook Formula Library
*Source: Lara Acosta + Justin Welsh*

| Hook Type | Template | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Specific Number | "I [did X] with [specific number]" | "I grew from 0 to 300K followers in 18 months. Here's what I'd do differently:" |
| Contrarian | "Stop [common advice]" | "Stop trying to go viral on LinkedIn." |
| Relatable Failure | "My [thing] looked like a failure until..." | "My first 200 posts averaged 87 likes. Here's what changed:" |
| POV | "POV: [specific scenario]" | "POV: You've been posting 3x a week for 6 months. Nothing is working." |
| Promise | "By the end of this, you'll know..." | "By the end of this post, you'll know exactly why your LinkedIn content isn't converting." |
| Contrast | "Most people do X. I do Y." | "Most B2B marketers chase MQLs. The best ones ignore them." |

**Rule:** Spend 30% of your writing time on the first line. It determines whether anyone reads the rest.

## Framework 4: The Dark Social Distribution Model
*Source: Chris Walker*

```
You post on LinkedIn
        ↓
ICP audience member sees it
        ↓
They share it in Slack / forward to a colleague / mention it in a meeting
        ↓
          [This part NEVER shows in your analytics]
        ↓
Warm prospect reaches out or accepts your DM
        ↓
Deal attributed to "direct" or "unknown" in your CRM
```

**Implication:** Do not optimize LinkedIn content for tracked clicks. Optimize for being *worth sharing* in private professional channels. The post that gets shared in a Slack group is worth more than the post that gets 500 likes.

## Framework 5: ICP-First Content Filter
*Source: Chris Walker + Ann Handley*

Before writing any post, answer all four questions:

1. **Who exactly** is this post for? (Job title, company size, specific problem)
2. **What do they currently believe** that this post will challenge or confirm?
3. **What do they want to do** after reading this? (Feel validated, share with their team, reach out)
4. **Why is this perspective only credible coming from us?** (What is our unique proof point?)

If you cannot answer all four, you are writing for yourself — not your buyer.

## Framework 6: The Founder-Led Content Flywheel
*Source: Dave Gerhardt*

```
Founder posts consistently about their expertise
              ↓
Relevant audience follows (CMOs, growth leaders, etc.)
              ↓
Community forms around shared problems
              ↓
Product or service offer emerges naturally from community needs
              ↓
Existing audience converts to customers
              ↓
Customer success stories → more content → larger audience
              ↓
              [Cycle repeats and compounds]
```

**Key insight:** Do not launch a LinkedIn content strategy to sell your product. Build an audience first by solving problems publicly. The product offer comes naturally later when the audience already trusts you.

## Framework 7: The BOFU Content Architecture
*Source: Lashay Lewis*

Most B2B SaaS content = TOFU (awareness). The highest-converting content is BOFU:

| Content Type | Buyer Stage | Example |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| Comparison page | Actively evaluating | "Our tool vs. Competitor — Honest Breakdown" |
| Use-case content | Considering fit | "How [job title] uses [product] to [outcome]" |
| ROI calculator | Justifying internally | "Calculate your ROI from switching to [product]" |
| Case study | Validating decision | "[Company] grew 3x after implementing [solution]" |

**LinkedIn application:** Repurpose BOFU insights as LinkedIn posts. A comparison insight becomes a carousel. A case study data point becomes a text post hook. One BOFU asset = multiple LinkedIn posts.

## Framework 8: The Content DNA Repurposing Engine
*Source: Devin Reed*

One research insight → 5 LinkedIn post formats:

1. **Long text post** — full argument, 400–600 words
2. **Carousel** — visual breakdown of the same idea, 6–10 slides
3. **Short hook post** — just the key insight distilled, 50–100 words
4. **Poll** — ask the audience if they experience the same thing
5. **Comment engagement** — drop the insight as a comment on someone else's relevant post

One insight = 5 posts = 2+ weeks of content = distributed across different reading habits and times.
