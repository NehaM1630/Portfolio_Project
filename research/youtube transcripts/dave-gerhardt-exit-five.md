## Video 1 — Dave Gerhardt: "How We Grew Exit Five to $1M ARR Through Content"

**Channel:** Exit Five — https://www.youtube.com/@ExitFive
**Published:** January 2026
**Duration:** 38 minutes

---

### Segment 1 [~4:30] — LinkedIn as the Primary Channel

Dave explains that 60% of Exit Five's growth came from LinkedIn alone. Not because he gamed the algorithm — because he showed up every single day with content genuinely useful to CMOs and marketing leaders.

"I was writing for one person: the B2B marketing leader who felt alone and under-resourced."

That specificity created strong audience identification. People didn't just follow him — they felt understood by him. That emotional connection is what converts followers into community members and eventually paying customers.

---

### Segment 2 [~11:20] — What Content Actually Converts

The posts that drove the most community sign-ups were not the ones with the most likes. They were posts that articulated a frustration the reader had but couldn't name.

"When someone reads your post and thinks 'this person gets it' — that's when they follow, join, or buy."

The most shareable content doesn't teach people something new. It gives language to something they already feel.

---

### Segment 3 [~18:45] — Founder-Led Content Is the Most Valuable Asset

A company page has roughly 3–5% of the organic reach of a personal profile. Every founder should be posting. The company's best LinkedIn asset is its founder's voice, not its brand account.

"If you're the CEO and you're not on LinkedIn, you're leaving your best marketing channel completely untouched."

At Drift, the brand grew because CEO David Cancel posted every day about what he was learning. The company content was secondary. The founder's voice was primary.

---

### Segment 4 [~25:10] — The Podcast-LinkedIn Repurposing System

Every Exit Five podcast episode becomes: 3 short video clips (posted to LinkedIn), 2 text post summaries, 1 newsletter feature. One 45-minute conversation becomes 6 pieces of content.

"We made it a system so it didn't depend on inspiration. Inspiration is not a content strategy."

---

### Segment 5 [~31:00] — How to Measure If LinkedIn Is Working

Dave's actual North Star metric: "How many DMs did I get this week from someone I didn't know?"

Not likes. Not follower count. Unsolicited DMs from relevant people = proof that content is reaching the right audience and creating genuine intent. Everything else is vanity.

## Video 2 — Dave Gerhardt: "B2B Marketing Strategy for 2026 — What's Changed"

**Channel:** Exit Five — https://www.youtube.com/@ExitFive
**Published:** December 2025
**Duration:** 44 minutes

---

### Segment 1 [~7:15] — The Death of the MQL

"The MQL as a primary success metric for marketing is dead for most B2B SaaS companies."

Buying committees are now larger (average 6.8 people in B2B SaaS decisions). Decisions take longer. Most of the research happens outside tracked channels. Optimizing for MQL leads to systematically underinvesting in organic content — the channel that actually influences decisions.

---

### Segment 2 [~19:30] — Brand vs. Performance: The Right Order

The best-performing B2B companies combine organic content (for brand and trust) with targeted paid (for demand capture). Neither works without the other. But the order matters: organic first, then paid to amplify.

"If you run ads to a cold audience that has never heard of you, you'll pay 4x what you'd pay if they'd already seen your content."

---

### Segment 3 [~28:45] — Why LinkedIn Beats Other Channels for B2B

LinkedIn outperforms other platforms for B2B because of audience composition (professionals in work mode), algorithm preference for native content, and professional context that makes people more receptive to business-relevant messages.

"Your buyer is on LinkedIn in work mode. They're not on other platforms thinking about your SaaS product."

## Video 3 — Chris Walker: "The Dark Social Framework for B2B SaaS"

**Channel:** Demand Gen Live — https://www.youtube.com/@ChrisWalker
**Published:** February 2026
**Duration:** 52 minutes

---

### Segment 1 [~5:00] — What Dark Social Actually Is

Dark social = all the conversations and content consumption that drives B2B purchasing decisions but never shows up in a CRM or attribution model.

Examples: a Slack DM where someone shares your LinkedIn post with a colleague. A group chat where a buyer says "I've been following this person for 6 months, I trust them." A team meeting where someone says "I saw on LinkedIn that..." These are unmeasurable but they represent the majority of how B2B buying decisions are actually influenced.

---

### Segment 2 [~14:20] — What This Means for LinkedIn Content Strategy

If most of the influence happens in unmeasurable dark social channels, then the job of LinkedIn content is to be worth sharing in those channels.

Post something so insightful that a CMO forwards it to their team in Slack. That is the goal — not getting a tracked click, not a LinkedIn like — but being shared privately among the people who buy from you.

---

### Segment 3 [~22:40] — Chris Walker's Posting System

Monday: identify the biggest piece of conventional wisdom in his space that he disagrees with.
Tuesday: write the post challenging it.
Wednesday: publish.
Thursday: read all comments, identify the 3–5 people who engaged most thoughtfully.
Friday: send each a personalized DM.

"The comment section isn't where the deal happens. It's where you find the people to start the real conversation with."

---

### Segment 4 [~35:00] — Scaling Beyond the Founder's Voice

When a B2B SaaS company crosses $10M ARR, the founder alone can no longer be the only LinkedIn voice. Build an "executive content program" — 3–5 leaders posting regularly.

"At Refine Labs, our five most senior people each posting 3x per week generated more pipeline than all of our paid LinkedIn ads combined."

The combined reach of multiple authentic personal voices outperforms any company page, at any follower count.
